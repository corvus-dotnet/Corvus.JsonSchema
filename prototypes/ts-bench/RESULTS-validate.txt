## Takeaway

validate-only is consistently FASTER than parse-only (~2x at every size) and triggers about HALF the minor
GCs over the same workload (small: 19 vs 42; large: 261 vs 359). The generated validator is therefore LEANER
than the unavoidable JSON.parse — it is not the allocation bottleneck on the validate path; parse-then-validate
GC pressure is dominated by JSON.parse itself. This refines the earlier "validator allocates ~as much as
JSON.parse" expectation: under sustained load the validator's own allocation is materially below parse's, so
there is no pressing validator-allocation cut to chase here. The realistic parse+validate path costs the sum
(gc-events add: small 42+19≈59, large 359+261≈627). Where end-to-end validate throughput matters most, the
lever is the parse, not the validator (and Model C RMW already sidesteps full re-parse on the write path).
